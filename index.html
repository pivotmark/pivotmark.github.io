The Pivot Mark 

The Pivot Mark () is a punctuation symbol used to mark the moment a thought changes direction. It doesn’t indicate a pause or a stop — it signals a deliberate shift in perspective.

Download Prolyn Font

Definition

The Pivot Mark represents a conceptual turn within a continuous sentence. It acknowledges one line of thinking and then redirects the reader toward another without breaking the flow.

What it looks like

The Pivot Mark has a distinct geometric form: an angled stroke with a short vertical foot, anchored by a square dot. This structure reflects its purpose — a deliberate shift in direction.

Examples in context

I thought I understood it  then I realized I didn’t.
We could follow the usual plan  or we could rethink everything.
It looked like a mistake at first  now it feels intentional.

Relation to other punctuation

  • Comma: continues the thought.
  • Dash: interrupts or emphasizes.
  • Semicolon: links related clauses.
  • Pivot Mark (): signals a shift in perspective.
